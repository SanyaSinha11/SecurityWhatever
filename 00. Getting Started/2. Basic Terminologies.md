# Initial Security Terms
In cybersecurity, a **threat** is the potential danger to your systems, a **vulnerability** is the weakness that can be exploited, an **exploit** is the specific method or tool used to abuse that weakness, and an **attack** is the broader, active execution of that exploit. 

## Threat
- **What it is:** 
  Any potential event, person, or circumstance—such as malware, a hacker, or a natural disaster—that has the potential to harm an organization or disrupt systems.
- **Analogy:** 
  A burglar walking down your street scoping out houses.
- **Key takeaway:** It is the _danger itself_.

## Vulnerability
- **What it is:** 
  A flaw, bug, or misconfiguration in a system's design or software that leaves it open to compromise.
- **Analogy:** 
  Leaving a window unlocked on the ground floor.
- **Key takeaway:** It is the _system's weakness_.

## Exploit 
- **What it is:** 
  A specific piece of code, software, or technique designed to take advantage of a specific vulnerability.
- **Analogy:** 
  Using a crowbar and a rock to force the unlocked window open and climb inside.
- **Key takeaway:** It is the _method or tool_ used to breach the weakness.

## Attack
- **What it is:** 
  The actual execution of hostile intent; the broader action of using exploits to compromise a target, steal data, or disrupt operations.
- **Analogy:** 
  The entire process of the burglar breaking into your house, stealing your valuables, and vandalizing the property.
- **Key takeaway:** It is the _active execution_ of the malicious activity.
